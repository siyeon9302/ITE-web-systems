Student ambassador 27th in Duksung Women’s University

Through my experience as a member of the “Bitnaemi” student ambassador team, I learned many valuable lessons. I gained a deeper understanding of how a large organization operates and developed a strong sense of responsibility for my work. As Vice President, I overcame my fear of speaking in front of others and cultivated attention to detail by providing final feedback on all content before publication. Since every post required approval from the president and vice president, I took great care to ensure that no inaccurate information was shared with students. This experience taught me to stay organized, review everything thoroughly, and approach all tasks with responsibility, diligence, and precision. Additionally, as a member of both the SNS and Design Teams, I became familiar with managing social media posts and designing card news using Canva. Because our mission was to promote the university, I consistently thought about how to represent our school in the best light—both internally and externally. Internally, I focused on sharing useful information with fellow students, while externally, I worked to highlight the strengths of our university to a broader audience. As a result of these efforts, our Instagram account surpassed 400 followers within one year, which was a rewarding achievement.

Grand Prize, 2022 Duksung Women’s University IT Engineering Hackathon

It was my first experience in collaboration — a hackathon. Duksung Women’s University operates an academic system where students explore various majors during their first year, so I only began learning my major courses as a sophomore. At the time of the hackathon, the knowledge I had was limited to what I learned in the first semester of my second year. The only practical experience I had was creating prototype screens using Adobe XD, and I didn’t even fully understand how the screens I designed were actually implemented through development. Nevertheless, I joined the hackathon with a spirit of challenge, determined to try anything. Despite the difficulties, I did my best. Our team consisted of four members — I was the only designer, and the other three were developers. The hackathon lasted for a week, and on Wednesday morning, one of the developers had to leave the project due to personal reasons. Because of this, it became difficult for the development team to continue with our original app idea, so we decided to switch to one of our earlier ideas. Since I was more familiar with mobile apps, designing a course registration website was a new challenge for me. Still, I did my best by researching various references. Although our communication between design and development wasn’t perfect, the experience was very meaningful as my first collaboration, and I was able to fully immerse myself in the project within a short time.

Design, KUSITMS 26th (Korea University Student IT & Management Association)

The first project was a collaboration with a company called Ecube Labs, where we were assigned to define problems in the company’s service and design wireframes as a task. Through this second collaborative experience, I realized the importance of clearly defining problems and the value of logical, well-reasoned planning and design. This project allowed me to experience collaboration with planners to the fullest extent. The second project was a meet-up project where participants selected their own topics and developed a web or mobile app over the course of about three months. Our team planned and developed a financial challenge app called “Tikkle”, targeting people in their 20s who wish to reflect on their spending habits, record their consumption, share their progress with others, and build sustainable financial habits. I was in charge of design, and through this third genuine collaboration, I came to understand what it means for the screens I design to be actually implemented by the frontend. Although my design skills were still developing, receiving practical feedback helped me deepen my understanding of design. It was also my first time using Figma, and I came to appreciate the usefulness of components. However, due to time constraints, I felt that the overall completeness of the project could have been higher.
